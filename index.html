Canada's top priority:
Climate Change


One of the most important issues for both Canada and the world is rising temperatures.
A wide range of factors, including heat related health issues, infrastructure, food
production, and economy, can be impacted by rising temperatures. In addition, populations
and ecosystems may be significantly affected by the severity and frequency of extreme weather
events brought on by climate change. To reduce the effects of climate change, and encourage
sustainable development, Canada needs to take immediate and effective actions.

Social Impacts


Firstly, climate change can have social impacts on Canadians. Many Canadians' lives are
impacted by rising temperatures and climate change. For example, longer and more intense
heat waves are associated with increased heat related illnesses and deaths, especially
amongst vulnerable populations, such as the elderly and those with existing health conditions.
Additionally, extended heat also increases the demand for cooling, increasing electricity
costs in summer, and the risk of food and water-borne contamination. On top of that, warmer
temperatures can allow the spread of forest and agricultural pests and disease-vectors (such
as ticks) into new regions. Hotter and drier conditions increase the risk of droughts and
wildfires. All of these facts have huge impacts on Canada's sustainability and Canadians
because these impacts can make it difficult for communities to sustain themselves. In conclusion,
these events can cause political instability, damage to the Canadian economy, and cause harm and loss
of life to Canadian citizens, which is why it is the most important issue facing Canada.


Environmental Impacts


Secondly, climate change can have an oustanding impact on the Canadian environment. Droughts, wildfires,
and extreme rainfall are happening more frequently as a direct result of rising temperatures.
These effects can be costly to the Canadian economy and ruin the natural beauty of this country.
In addition, it is unsustainable because it has a direct impact on Canada's food production,
making Canada more reliant on other countries for food. On the other hand, global sea level has
risen about 8 inches (0.2 meters) since reliable record-keeping began in 1880. By 2100, scientists
project that it will rise at least another foot (0.3 meters), but possibly as high as 8 feet (2.4 meters),
if we continue carbon emissions at our current rate. Rising sea levels can negatively impact coastal
communities by flooding their territory, causing expensive damage to the economy. Furthermore, we
already see effects scientists predicted, such as the loss of sea ice, melting glaciers and ice
sheets, sea level rise, and more intense heat waves. Taken as a whole, the range of published evidence
indicates that the net damage costs of climate change are likely to be significant and to increase over
time. These impacts also impact sustainability because it is affecting food production, and these impacts
can make it difficult for people in coastal communities to live sustainably.


Economical Impacts

Climate change can also affect the economy. Rising temperatures show a significant threat to the
Canadian economy, with a wide range of factors that are expected to negatively impact our economy.
These include damage to infrastructures, increased cost of services, and risks to financial stability.
Furthermore, the effects of climate change can be expected to shave 11 percent to 14 percent off global
economic output by 2050 compared with growth levels without climate change, according to a report from
Swiss Re, one of the world's largest providers of insurance to other insurance companies. That amounts
to as much as $23 trillion in reduced annual global economic output worldwide as a result of climate
change. The effects of climate change are also expected to be felt in the form of decreased productivity.
will continue to have a huge effect on our economy through potential reductions in our productivity and
increases to infrastructure and service costs. By 2061, it is projected that between 700,000 and 2.7
million more days of work will be lost every year because climate change will cause more frequent and
intense heat waves. Additionally, it is not sustainable for society as it is not utilizing the full
potential of the workforce and resources. In conclusion, climate change presents a range of risks and
impacts that are expected to negatively impact our economy. These include property loss and damage,
infrastructure and service costs and risks to financial stability. It is crucial for Canada and the
world to take action to mitigate the effects of climate change and implement sustainable practices to
protect our economy and society from these negative impacts.


Conclusion


In conclusion, rising temperature is one of the most important issues facing Canada today. It affects
not only the environment and ecosystems, but also human health, the economy, and the workforce. As
temperatures rise, we can expect to see more severe weather events, increased costs to repair and
maintain infrastructure, and lost productivity due to heat related health problems. Rising temperatures
and extreme weather events are already having a significant impact on our economy, environment, and
communities. From droughts and wildfires, to loss of biodiversity, the effects of climate change are
already being felt across Canada. But it will only get worse if we don't take immediate and decisive
actions. The future of Canada depends on our ability to transition to a sustainable, low-carbon country.
